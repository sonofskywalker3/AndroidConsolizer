# Android Consolizer 3.7.0 — Bug Fix Release 2

A focused follow-up to 3.6.0: two controller-handling fixes and a round of log-noise cleanup. Smaller than 3.6.0 by design — these are targeted fixes, each individually toggleable via GMCM.

## Bug fixes

**Picked-up items land in the toolbar row you're actually using.** Pick up a piece of furniture, grab forage, receive a gift, or buy from a shop while your second or third toolbar row is showing — the item now goes into *that* row, instead of always dropping into row 0 where you can't see it. Furniture, which stays your held "tool" until you place it again, also moves the selection to follow it; ordinary pickups never disturb whatever tool you currently have selected. New GMCM toggle: **Pickup To Active Row** (on by default).

**Triggers no longer skip two toolbar slots.** On Gamesir and G Cloud controllers, a single trigger pull would occasionally jump two slots instead of one. The cause: hall-effect triggers briefly read zero partway through a pull, and some digital triggers glitch for a single frame — either way the old detector saw it as a second press. The trigger handling is now a two-threshold state machine with a short release-confirmation window that rides through those dropouts. Verified on both an analog (G Cloud) and a digital (Gamesir X2) controller.

## Cleanup

**Less log spam.** Leftover `[Bed]` and `[StartHold]` diagnostic lines from the 3.5-era bed-bouncing and Quest Log investigations are downgraded to Debug level and gated behind the Verbose Logging toggle — your SMAPI log stays quiet during normal play.

## Behind the scenes

Two long-standing questions were closed by investigation, with no code change needed:

- The **bed-bouncing** fix from 3.6.0 was confirmed structurally correct — bed removal on Android is asynchronous, so gating the downstream removal step (rather than the upstream `canBeRemoved` check) is the only reliable place to stop it. Farm Type Manager was ruled out as a contributor.
- The reported **Xbox/PS X/Y button overlap** ("Y both picks up an item and sorts the container") could not be reproduced on current code — it was resolved incidentally by input-pipeline rework since 3.4.

Full technical write-ups are in `DONE.md`.

## Compatibility & toggles

Every fix is a separate GMCM toggle. If something conflicts with another mod or doesn't suit your setup, disable it individually in the in-game GMCM config menu.
